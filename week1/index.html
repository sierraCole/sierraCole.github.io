Long Live the Web

  • “The Web as we know it, however, is being threatened...”
  • “You do not have to get approval from any central authority to add a page or make a link.”
  • “The primary design principle underlying the Web’s usefulness is universality.”

My Response

When reading Long Live the Web, a lot of what Lee is saying in 2010 feels even more relevant now. This aspect of digital exclusivity, whether it be with social media, shopping websites (like Amazon), Apple, or YouTube, is quite alarming to me. Certain paid features on these websites, or even companies moving from websites to apps for further exclusiveness, speaks to how the internet itself is becoming a capitalist structure.

I will push back a little bit by saying I don’t think Lee is taking into account the harmful things on the internet. His point about predators using URIs to stalk people is a valid one, however, URIs can also be used to catch predators who use the internet to find victims or access predatory content.
